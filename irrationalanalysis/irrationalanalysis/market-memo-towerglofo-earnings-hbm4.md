Here is the actual process of HBM4 qualification from an engineering perspective.

  1. Nvidia measures the s-parameters of their Rubin CoWoS-L package using samples from TSMC and a several-million dollar machine called a PNA from Keysight. (the competition in this segment is dogshit… has to be Keysight)




[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ewso!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9fd44e9b-c1eb-43b8-9dea-58ec667a94ba_1348x1124.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ewso!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9fd44e9b-c1eb-43b8-9dea-58ec667a94ba_1348x1124.png)

  2. Nvidia systems engineers combine the data and various simulations to make a golden s-parameter model for the channel. It’s just a text file.

  3. Micron/Samsung/Hynix uses the provided golden s-parameter model to conduct necessary R&D for their HBM4 logic base die.

  4. In order to test their HBM4 stacks, the memory makers must use **PROBE STATIONS** because they don’t have the actual CoWoS package. **PROBE STATIONS** are notoriously difficult to calibrate. You have to measure the s-parameters of the probes themselves very carefully and de-embed (back out) the probe response. Contact variation can fuck up measurements. Not getting your ground reference right can fuck up measurements. 

  5. It is **VERY EASY** to botch high-speed testing using probe setups. **There is a reason people avoid probes unless absolutely necessary.**

  6. Micron/Samsung/Hynix test their HBM4 and think everything is fine. Meeting Nvidia aggressive 11 Gbps per pin spec. Ship samples to TSMC.

  7. TSMC packages Nvidia test dice with HBM4 sample test stacks using CoWoS-L.

  8. Nvidia tests and inevitably finds problems because shit like this never works first attempt.

  9.  **Nvidia and HBM4 vendor collaborate to try and tune both the PHY (on Rubin chip) and the HBM4 logic base die to get it to work at 11 Gbps per pin.**

  10.  **BOTH SIDES HAVE HUNDREDS OF SECRET TUNING REGISTERS THAT THEY NEED TO USE BUT DO NOT WANT TO DISCLOSE TO OTHER PARTY. SIGNIFICANT BACK-AND-FORTH USING ASYMETRIC INFORMATION NEEDED TO TUNE.**




I hope you now understand why analysis based on supply-chain bullshit does not work in this case.

It is very easy for HBM4 vendors to screw up internal testing with high-speed probe setups. Not their fault. This shit is difficult.

The level of asymmetry of information between Nvidia and HBM4 vendor (works both ways) is very high. Both sides want to keep their secrets but still need to collaborate to have any hope of getting any of this shit to work at all.
