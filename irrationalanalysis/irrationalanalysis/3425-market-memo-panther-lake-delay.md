Who is the target audience for this marketing? Other Qualcomm employees?

Let’s go one by one.

  1. Sub-6 Bandwidth

    1. How much sub-6GHz (useful 5G) spectrum a modem can process.

    2. Carriers rarely allocate so much spectrum to a single user.

    3. 160 MHz is perfectly fine. 

    4. **98% of iPhone 16e users will not notice this.**

  2. Downlink Carrier Aggregation

    1. Intuitively, how many cell towers your phone can connect to at one time.

    2. This is needed for handover (user moving out of range of a tower) and coverage.

    3. 3x DL CA is good enough. 

    4. Again, wireless service providers (real-world networks) will not allocate tons of carriers to a single user who does not need it.

    5.  **98% of iPhone 16e users will not notice this.**

  3. Uplink MIMO

    1. How many data layers (via antenna diversity) can be used in uplink.

    2. This actually does matter for people who like to upload videos and photos to social media over cellular data.

  4. Switched Uplink

    1. Using uplink slots on a TDD and FDD band at the same time.

    2. Unclear if the Apple C1 modem is so shit, it does not support 3GPP release 16 switched uplink… lol.

    3. Not supporting release 17 switched uplink enhancements is expected given how most of the worlds infra is still on release 15/16. 

  5. 6 Rx

    1. Nobody cares.

    2. It blows up BOM cost due to more RFFE.

    3. Most Android OEMs skip this.

    4. Cope harder buddy.

  6. mmWave

    1. Nobody cares.

    2. mmWave was a commercial failure in 2019 and it is still a commercial failure.
