What actually happened was completely different and very surprising.

  1. Agreement for Nvidia to use IFS advanced packaging (Foveros).

    * Appears to be second source of CoWoS-L style packaging.

    * (there is some ambiguity because Intel uses “Foveros” branding for multiple technologies.

  2. Intel and Nvidia to collaborate on an x86 NVLink fusion CPU for future (Rubin Ultra?) AI racks.

  3. Intel and Nvidia to collaborate on laptop chips using Nvidia graphics chiplet connected via NVlink.

  4. Nvidia now owns 4.9% of Intel. They paid for these shares unlike Trump who simply changed chips act grants into equity. 




#1 is a great win. IFS needed to get SOMETHING and advanced packaging is the easiest entry point. Hopefully the retards in that group will finally remove head from ass and deliver reasonable design rules and customer support.

#2 is a HUGE win and makes a lot of sense. There are ongoing issues with Grace (ARM-based) acting as a serious bottleneck due to shit performance. Kerneles cannot get launched fast enough to keep the GPUs fed. The joke that Nvidia Grace is “the worlds most expensive memory controller” is true. Chip is dogshit because of the ARM Neoverse V2 CPU cores. 

**Nvidia is working on fully custom CPU core design for Vera and this x86 project with Intel seems to be a hedge.**

In the past, Nvidia has tried to design their own ARM-based custom CPU architecture and it sucked.

<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Project_Denver>

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1tY5!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9ed55949-4fab-4530-92e2-4f63ec762796_333x930.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1tY5!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9ed55949-4fab-4530-92e2-4f63ec762796_333x930.png)

Denver 1 (2014), Denver 2 (2016), and Carmel (2018) were all trash. It is very smart of Jensen to have a backup plan or at least an alternative in case custom cores for Vera suck.

#3 is for laptops and I frankly don’t care but other people are asking so here is the short version.

This does nothing for Nvidia. It seems to just be a deal sweetener.

Intel needs to cut more OpEx and the graphics division is a prime target. I have been arguing for years that the entire Intel graphics group at Intel should have been cut down to the bone just to keep integrated GPU in laptop chips alive.

It looks like Lip-Bu Tan’s long-term (within 3-5 years) plan is to cut the entire division. 

Within Windows integrated graphics, there are only three options right now:

  * Intel (bad but functions)

  * AMD (pretty good)

  * Qualcomm (unusable for anything 3D)




Nvidia graphics (in an integrated form) would crush all of the above but they need a competent partner to help with the boring stuff. They busy with datacenter and don’t have time for this peanut-scale market.

MedaTek was supposed to be that partner but that relationship appears to have gone south.

Imagine a future where a kickass Nvidia GPU chiplet is hybrid-bonded on top of an Intel CPU to create the worlds best laptop chip. 

That future is coming. The remaining employees within Intel’s graphics department will not be part of this future.

Nvidia is buying Enfabrica. This is surprising to me.

Let’s take a look at Enfabrica’s Hot Chips 2024 presentation.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!CKUG!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9517976a-09b5-480a-b12d-838a74f463b3_1231x684.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!CKUG!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9517976a-09b5-480a-b12d-838a74f463b3_1231x684.png)

It’s basically a hybrid switch with both Ethernet and PCIe. Never understood the appeal of this.

Replacing all network cards in the system with one super-NIC-switch-thing seems cool on paper but kills a lot of the flexibility when trying to configure a network topology.

Curious to see what Nvidia does with this IP. There are benefits to the Enfabrica approach if the entire system is designed around their concept. Maybe this becomes something real in Feynmann AI rack.
