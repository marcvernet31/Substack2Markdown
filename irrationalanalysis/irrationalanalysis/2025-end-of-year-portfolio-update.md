There are many “analysts” out there who are often trying to sell you something. Investment banking services, consulting fees, models, subscriptions, expert calls, sponsorships…

 **I don’t make a single penny from this newsletter.** My two sources of income are engineering dayjob and hobby trading/gambling.

Many have offered to pay me and I have refused every time. Only allow hedge fund friends to pay for dinner from time to time so cumulative maybe < $1K in free food/drink each year.

I strongly believe that traditional “compliance” regimes where analysts are severely restricted or outright prohibited from trading securities is stupid. My form of compliance is much more logical.

  * All my positions (every penny to my name) are regularly disclosed with average price.

  * I am very transparent about what I plan to do in terms of investing ideas.

  *  **My opinion will never be altered due to financial bias.** I always say whatever I think, even if it is financially detrimental to my own holdings. Those of you who talk to me privately know this to be true.

  * I am a hobbyist gambling with my own money. What you do with your money is not my problem. By not accepting subscription money, I wash my hands of any liability.




Due to rules regarding my dayjob, I cannot accept money for anything related to said dayjob. Writing about semiconductor stocks is very related.

So my policy of refusing payment in any form (excluding occasional meal) stays.

Substack has a “pledge” feature where you can enter your credit card information to indicate if you are willing to pay me in the future if I decide to enable payments.

 ****I will never paywall anything.** The free and open internet is a cherished resource and I feel obligated to contribute back.** Honestly I have spent more time on the internet than going outside and doing stuff in real life. How do you think I learned all this shit? Thousands upon thousands of hours crawling the internet obsessing over everything related to semiconductors.

The pledge thing is enabled because as the newsletter grows, the probability I get doxxed, fired, and blacklisted from industry goes up. A problematic number of people know my real name.

If you would like to pledge a donation to the “Irrational Analysis got fired” fund, go ahead. You shall receive literally nothing in return. It’s a pledge to donate in the future. Don’t give a shit if you do. Also I will give ample warning before enabling subscriptions if the time comes where I no longer have an engineering salary.
