# [1] Basic Technical Info

For a general technical primer on switching regulators, check out this old post.

In short, switching regulators (typically) convert high voltage DC into a lower voltage DC in an efficient manner using a chonkey transistor and some analog filtering.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OFq5!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F29586b4c-8b36-4c2f-80de-1c7358721b3c_2895x1570.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OFq5!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F29586b4c-8b36-4c2f-80de-1c7358721b3c_2895x1570.png)

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vE5h!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5422974d-baad-4fc4-8ab0-d6210b09efc5_835x631.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vE5h!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5422974d-baad-4fc4-8ab0-d6210b09efc5_835x631.png)

Switching regulators add noise. It’s intrinsic to the concept.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!m3vU!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9ff04847-b454-48ef-9127-4a0e7c14f2fd_1321x691.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!m3vU!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9ff04847-b454-48ef-9127-4a0e7c14f2fd_1321x691.png)

Here is a great example:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!A0SK!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff8db8a7e-9080-436d-85af-5ce7669d1512_864x643.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!A0SK!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff8db8a7e-9080-436d-85af-5ce7669d1512_864x643.png)

Yellow is input DC voltage to the switching regulator. (clean)

Purple is the output DC voltage of the switching regulator with some nasty AC noise.

Green is the filtered (cleaned up) voltage to whatever this system is supposed to power.

Despite the noise issues, switching regulators are very attractive because of their high conversion efficiency. The efficiency curve always looks something like this.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1VCf!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F30364953-4c46-4ff5-81ec-9a47b16f4441_1344x679.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1VCf!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F30364953-4c46-4ff5-81ec-9a47b16f4441_1344x679.png)<https://www.monolithicpower.com/en/documentview/productdocument/index/version/2/document_type/Datasheet/lang/en/sku/MP86972GLJTH/>

This particular [MPWR 0.00%↑](https://substack.com/discover/stocks/MPWR) “60 amp” switching regulator has optimal efficiency at 15A load current. In fact, the efficiency is crap at 60A.

It is very common for power delivery network designers to over-build the system to deliver better efficiency at typical load, while adding safety margin.

Modern switching regulators are expected to have a wide variety of standard safety features. The alternative is accepting the risk of components going kaboom or self-immolating or frying your expensive GPU.

# [2] What does Navitas do?

Navitas makes switching regulators out of specialty materials such as Gallium Nitride (GaN) and Silicon Carbide (SiC).

Their website does a great job of explaining the technical reasons behind these materials.

Also, [Vikram Sekar](https://open.substack.com/users/124411709-vikram-sekar?utm_source=mentions) of [Vik's Newsletter](https://open.substack.com/pub/viksnewsletter) has several great posts on semiconductor material science and power semis.

When you turn a chip on, there is some inrush current.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!g1go!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd62549c8-e65d-4cbf-889a-eb302486fcee_693x446.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!g1go!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd62549c8-e65d-4cbf-889a-eb302486fcee_693x446.jpeg)

As the chip operates, it draws variable current/voltage/power which places strain on the switching regulators. 

Bad switching regulators cause performance stability and can even fry your expensive GPU.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3a_O!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc9dbf2e3-fcec-48b5-8346-628c8dff6a6c_890x599.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3a_O!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc9dbf2e3-fcec-48b5-8346-628c8dff6a6c_890x599.png)Note that this is for a gaming GPU. The datacenter GPUs draw way more power. <https://www.igorslab.de/en/nvidia-pcat-in-test-convenient-power-acquisition-measurement-and-data-acquisition-from-frame-time-to-system-data/3/>

The investment case for Navitas is simple.

They ****CLAIM**** to have GaN switching regulators with sufficient safety features that make them viable in datacenter applications.

Normally, the datasheet has a load current vs efficiency plot that uses some assumptions for output filtering capacitors and temperature. RDS_ON depends on temperature.

Navitas does not have the plot I am looking for. They do have a press release on efficiency.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Rr3m!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb43acdbf-56dd-4ba2-b361-fee91883a454_1378x1138.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Rr3m!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb43acdbf-56dd-4ba2-b361-fee91883a454_1378x1138.png)<https://navitassemi.com/navitas-launches-industry-leading-12kw-gan-sic-platform-achieving-97-8-efficiency-for-hyperscale-ai-data-centers/>

97.8% efficiency is meaningfully better than the competition. Monolithic Power’s best parts get 95% efficiency under ideal conditions.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3Pnz!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa5f40e19-a74f-4c89-bacf-bd42281d4e44_914x503.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3Pnz!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa5f40e19-a74f-4c89-bacf-bd42281d4e44_914x503.jpeg)

This 100A rated high-end MPS part gets 95% efficiency at 30A load current and 25C ambient temperature with an external 3.3 Vcc. Obviously, an external 3.3 Vcc will harm overall system efficiency.
