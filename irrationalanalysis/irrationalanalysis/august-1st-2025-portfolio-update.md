This was totally fine. My perception of risk is very abnormal and quite dark.

Most normal people would be concerned by losing money. My primary concern has always ego-related. Given how much I know about semiconductors, I should be crushing the market at all times. 

Money is an artificial construct. Winning is real and money is simply an easy to measure and universally accepted proxy for winning.

Put another way, imagine a race. Most people want to win the race. Usually, to achieve the satisfaction that their hard work paid off.

I am the kind of person who wants to win by such a high margin that I can stop right before the finish line and turn around. Watch everyone else struggle and lose.

This probably reflects poorly upon my character… but why lie? I derive most of my self-worth from being right and (more importantly) knowing and proving that others are wrong.

I am not a good person. Surprise!
