## [1] Advanced Packaging Absolute Basics

Semianalysis has an excellent [5-part series](https://semianalysis.com/2022/01/06/advanced-packaging-part-2-review/) on advanced packaging. In the interest of time, I will only explain the bare minimum information for you to understand this long/short idea.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!oKbX!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F56ac5901-1e23-4809-9f8e-c249d86f5ab5_1017x531.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!oKbX!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F56ac5901-1e23-4809-9f8e-c249d86f5ab5_1017x531.png)

Let’s focus on CoWoS varients.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!d-mO!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F673f1819-b7f0-48f8-8a72-273b0f808a51_1920x1008.webp)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!d-mO!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F673f1819-b7f0-48f8-8a72-273b0f808a51_1920x1008.webp)

CoWoS-S is the simplest version. Not many routing options.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!igQw!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2d655eb5-eac5-4d6a-bb5d-8b779afca2fe_1024x576.webp)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!igQw!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2d655eb5-eac5-4d6a-bb5d-8b779afca2fe_1024x576.webp)

CoWoS-R is more complex. It provides a re-routing (re-distribution) layer and better performance when compared to CoWoS-S.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!bbai!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F00853e36-7e17-4d05-9723-c97e76ea50af_1024x576.webp)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!bbai!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F00853e36-7e17-4d05-9723-c97e76ea50af_1024x576.webp)

CoWoS-L is an enhanced version of CoWoS-R where tiny silicon bridges (local silicon interconnects) are embedded into the package RDL.

For those of you wondering about Intel Foundry, TSMC CoWoS-L ~= Intel EMIB…

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xXoM!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F714a5f65-7911-48fb-a73e-819ac216d3c2_1200x527.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xXoM!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F714a5f65-7911-48fb-a73e-819ac216d3c2_1200x527.png)

Conceptually, these are very similar technologies from an **end users perspective.**

Yes, the packaging process and materials science are technically different.

No, I don’t care.

Let’s talk about bump-pitch.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!INM0!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F87b8459c-0b3e-4b82-8159-7560350d0cb0_2930x1993.avif)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!INM0!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F87b8459c-0b3e-4b82-8159-7560350d0cb0_2930x1993.avif)

Bump-pitch is how far apart each I/O pad is from it’s nearest neighbor.

Bump-size (diameter) is how big the I/O pad + solder ball is.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!IKdt!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9e602640-c646-4e23-a728-94de272ee27a_3466x2171.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!IKdt!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9e602640-c646-4e23-a728-94de272ee27a_3466x2171.png)

The main problem with advanced packaging is aligning all the chips (logic dice, HBM dice) with super small bump-pitch. 

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!WZNg!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F32f3c23f-0b5c-478b-9c70-a4613381dd2e_2381x1304.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!WZNg!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F32f3c23f-0b5c-478b-9c70-a4613381dd2e_2381x1304.png)

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Wq19!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff181175c-6454-4b09-ba4a-a6a9d606e89c_500x563.webp)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Wq19!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff181175c-6454-4b09-ba4a-a6a9d606e89c_500x563.webp)

[For a deeper dive into what goes wrong in advanced packages, check out this semiengineering article.](https://semiengineering.com/what-goes-wrong-in-advanced-packages/)

In short, this shit is very difficult and mistakes are expensive. This is why advanced packaging inspection companies (Onto, Camtek) benefitted so much with the AI boom.
