When I first entered the workforce full-time, my base salary was $85K/year.

At the time, I lived in a two-bedroom apartment with a total rent of approximately 2.5K/month, of which I paid 1.5K/month as I had the master bedroom.

My total post-tax annual spend was 40-50K/year. Did not have much money to invest because maxing-out my 401K left very little free money. Was only able to put a few hundred dollars into my ROTH IRA (was poor enough to have one of those) every month.

Like many engineers, I made the mistake of believing in my employer. Believing in ESPP and RSU (company stock grants). Believing that my work had economic value. Believing I could make a difference.

Corporations are artificial constructs that exist to distribute risk and profit. Nothing more.

The thing is, every company pitches itself as a “family”. Something more that what it actually is. I have bounced around the tech sector and seen the same shit.

In reality, you (the employee) are nothing more than a number on a spreadsheet.

Here is a fun HR trick.

Take you base salary and multiply by 1.2-1.5x. That is a rough estimate of how much you cost to the employer once benefits (healthcare, 401K, ect) are accounted for in a cash-basis. 

You are not human. The moment shit goes south, someone from the professional-managerial class will bring up the spreadsheet and decide who to eject so they can save their own skin.

I have seen people loyal to an employer for 20+ years only to be discarded overnight.

I have seen useless middle-management (Director // Sr. Dir, VP) accomplish literally nothing and simply hire people to get promoted, then fire the same people a year or two later to save themselves.

I have seen teams where 70% or more of the employees have zero to negative productivity, because politics matter more than engineering objectives or basic financial strategy/logic.

I have seen very good, kind, exceptionally smart, ethical people made so miserable that they leave. 10+ years of hard work and loyalty only to be pushed out after a re-org and high-school-level political bickering.

I have seen some shit. There are many reasons I am so jaded.
