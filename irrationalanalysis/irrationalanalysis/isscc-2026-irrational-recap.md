This is using TSMC COUPE. An optical extension of NVLink D2D.

D2D PHYs (UCIe style) rely on clock forwarding. Sending the clock optically is the main challenge. 

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tZgK!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F57f69b81-ddfb-4f86-ac0d-c10eecabde4e_1583x821.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tZgK!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F57f69b81-ddfb-4f86-ac0d-c10eecabde4e_1583x821.png)

This slide is very important because it shows one of the main issues with ring modulators. 

Periodicity.

Any junk outside of the black box in the center can show up. Optical bandpass filters are very difficult to make.

Additionally, notice two key attributes of the system…

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HXtA!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0531a984-9dd0-4863-8ac6-a2cb2b8084dd_757x422.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HXtA!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0531a984-9dd0-4863-8ac6-a2cb2b8084dd_757x422.png)

Grating couplers are wonderful for manufacturing but induce a bandwidth and power penalty. Losing 4 dB just from coupling is painful. Edge coupling is more 1-1.5 dB if done right but much more expensive and scales poorly.

The ring extinction ratio could be 15 dB if modulation aligns with the resonance. Reality is very different. Will get to that shortly.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0XaW!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F16203bc9-19c3-4f72-8506-7ab6fb5c219e_1564x818.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0XaW!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F16203bc9-19c3-4f72-8506-7ab6fb5c219e_1564x818.png)

  1. Averaging should never be used in this context. Scope averaging is for getting noise floor. 

  2. Anything less than PRBS13 should not be used.

  3. Extinction ratio of 3.6 dB is not great. Most non-ring modulators can hit 5-9 dB ER.




Bluntly, this Tx eye is not impressive. They seem to have no margin on amplitude noise.
